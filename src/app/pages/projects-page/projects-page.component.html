Batteries Included

It's the late 90s and Randy and Sarah are just like any other kid. The two siblings live with their mom and dad in a quaint corner of suburbia. And just like other children the two siblings have a colorful collection of toys, from dolls to homemade knick knacks to bombastic action figures. But unknown to both children their toys have a life of their own, and they don't always get along! Randy's toys, led by Will E. Rust a trusty robot sheriff, and Sarah's toys, led by Ms. Poppy a cute but killer plush bunny, live at odds with one another but enjoy a relative sense of peace (much like most siblings). Despite their differences, Randy's and Sarah's toys have a sort of peace treaty among them: whenever the family is away from home, the two groups watch TV together. One Friday evening the family is out on their own ritual: "weekend family together time" at the local pizza and arcade down the street. Seeing their opportunity, the toys rush to the downstairs living room to enjoy their usual ritual of watching TV. While bickering over what to watch, the toys notice a new commercial for a brand new toy store called the Toy Factory. As the commercial explains, the Toy Factory both manufactures toys and directly sells them to happy kids everywhere. Boasting cheap prices and excellent craftsmanship, the Toy Factory announces that it is opening next weekend. Coincidentally, next weekend happens to be Randy's birthday, but aside from that the toys believe this to be unimportant and resume their bickering. Not long after, however, the toys hear the family car pulling back into the driveway and make their way upstairs to return to their normal resting spots. After hearing the excited murmurs from Randy and Sarah downstairs, the toys soon discover the family did not just go to their usual hangout spot but were actually at a special promotional campaign for the Toy Factory. That's not all, as part of this campaign the family was given a special toy: a new and rare Spy toy. The toys quickly welcome this new Spy toy into their home, dazzled with his plethora of accessories. From plastic rocket launchers to a fully functional walkie talkie and even glow in the dark effects, it seems like this toy has it all! But as it turns out this Spy toy is in fact...a toy spy! His mission: to break all the toys in the house. Despite his best attempts, Spy proves unable to break a single toy in the house (much to his embarrassment) and his plot is soon discovered by the toys of the house. With Spy captured, the gang discover that he is merely a henchman of the true villain: The Supreme Molder who's true mission is to break every toy in the world so that kids everywhere will be forced to buy replacements at the Toy Factory. With his toys in place, Molder plans to take over the entire world! Having a change of heart, Spy and the rest of the toys team up to stop Molder from ruining Randy's birthday party (and to a lesser extent save the world).
